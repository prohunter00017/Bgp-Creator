How to Play

Welcome, young explorers, to the thrilling world of Geometry Dash Kenos! This super fun and challenging level is a real adventure, packed with exciting twists and turns. Let's dive into how you can navigate through this amazing world and become a Geometry Dash hero!

Control Your Icon

  • Jump: Use the left-click button, the spacebar, or the up arrow key to make your icon jump. Timing is key, so practice makes perfect!
  • Fly: Hold down the jump key to ascend and release it to descend. This is especially useful in the ship sections where you need to navigate through tight spaces.
  • Rings: Press the jump key when your character touches rings to activate special effects like size changes or dash boosts.
  • Pads: Your icon will automatically jump when touching pads, making it easier to move through certain sections.

85-92% (Pennutoh): Slow down in a new cube section. Navigate through spikes, saw-blades, and thorns, hitting orbs. Follow this with a mini-ship, small ball, and gravity portals. Repeat with a cube and mini-wave segment, ending with a wave segment between saw-blades.

Skill Progression & Difficulty Curve

Welcome to the thrilling world of geometry-dash-kenos, young explorers! This Extreme Demon level is like a rollercoaster ride through a series of amazing worlds. Each section is crafted by different creators, making it a unique and exciting adventure. Let's dive into the skill progression and difficulty curve of this epic level!

As you start geometry-dash-kenos, you'll jump into the Eclipsed section (0-7%). Here, you'll control a cube, zooming through walls and hitting orbs while dodging spikes. The gravity changes keep you on your toes, and a quick mini-ball segment adds a twist. It's a fast-paced start to get your adrenaline pumping!

Next, you'll enter the Zimnior12 section (7-14%). This part introduces the mini-UFO, where you'll need to hit orbs with precision. Then, you'll switch back to the cube and robot, jumping over walls and hitting orbs. The challenge ramps up as you navigate through these tricky sections.

The Bianox section (14-31%) brings you back to the cube, similar to the start of Sakupen Hell. You'll jump on walls, hitting blue orbs quickly. A mini-wave section follows, and then a mini-ball section where you must move swiftly between walls without touching spikes. The wave section with inclined obstacles and gravity portals will test your skills even further.

In the Terron section (31-36%), you'll face a lengthy UFO segment. Navigate through narrow spaces between spikes, jumping carefully between them, hitting orbs, and traversing gravity and size portals. It's a delicate dance that requires precision.

The Lazuli section (36-45%) has you flying as a ship through obstacles like spikes and saw-blades. Managing gravity changes and hitting orbs at the right time is crucial. A mini-cube section and wave segment between spiky inclined walls follow, ending with a short UFO segment.

As you reach the KrampuX section (45-56%), you'll encounter a cube section with size changes. Carefully jump on walls and hit orbs through spikes. A mini-wave section, robot section, mini-ship section, and wave segment with moving walls and saw-blades follow, keeping you on your toes.

The SlimJim section (56-64%) transitions you into a mini-ship sequence. Maneuver around spike obstacles and blocks, hitting orbs, and changing gravity. A mini-robot segment, a mini-UFO bouncing around moving structures, and a mini-ship section between sliding walls with spikes add to the excitement.

In the Nikrodox section (64-68%), you'll navigate a short robot segment through spikes, walls, and blocks at half-speed. Make careful jumps in tight spaces to progress.

The ChiefFlurry section (68-74%) is a fast-paced wave segment. Maneuver through tight spaces with moving walls containing spikes. It transitions into a normal wave segment with a few moving blocks before reaching the cube portal.

The Salaxium section (74-80%) is a tight double-speed section with gravity changes, dash orbs, and challenging timings. Difficult timings, a dash orb spam section, and more challenging timings lead to a brief auto section.

In the Spectex section (80-85%), you'll control a wave, maneuvering between walls at narrow distances, changing size and gravity. The section is divided into two parts by another teleport portal.

The Pennutoh section (85-92%) slows down with a new cube section. Navigate through spikes, saw-blades, and thorns, hitting orbs along the way. A mini-ship sequence, a small ball section, and flying through gravity portals follow. A cube and mini-wave segment repeat, and the section ends with a wave segment maneuvering between saw-blades using gravity portals.

Finally, in the Splash section (92-100%), you'll enter a timing-based cube section, jumping through gravity portals with orbs. Transitioning into a ship sequence, navigate tight spaces between saw blades and walls. Using a green dash orb, you'll approach the end of the level, culminating with an end-screen created by Splash.

Each section of geometry-dash-kenos offers a unique challenge, pushing your skills to the limit. Are you ready to conquer this Extreme Demon level and become a true Geometry Dash master?

Common Mistakes (and Fixes)

Hey there, young explorers! Geometry Dash Kenos is an amazing adventure, but even the best players can get stuck. Here are some common mistakes you might run into and how to fix them.

1. Rushing Through Sections

One of the biggest mistakes is rushing through the sections. Remember, Geometry Dash Kenos is all about timing and precision. Take your time, especially in the fast-paced sections. For example, in the 0-7% (Eclipsed) section, you'll control a cube jumping on walls and hitting orbs. If you rush, you might miss an orb or hit a spike. Slow down, focus on each jump, and you'll get through it!

2. Ignoring Gravity Changes

Gravity changes can be tricky, but they're super important. In the 14-31% (Bianox) section, you'll face a mini-wave part with inclined obstacles and gravity portals. If you ignore the gravity changes, you'll end up hitting spikes or missing orbs. Pay close attention to the screen and adjust your jumps accordingly. Practice this section until you feel comfortable with the gravity shifts.

3. Not Using Dash Orbs

Dash orbs can be a lifesaver, especially in tight sections. In the 74-80% (Salaxium) section, you'll encounter a double-speed part with dash orbs and challenging timings. Use these orbs to zip through tough spots. If you see a dash orb, don't hesitate to use it! It can help you avoid spikes and reach orbs that are hard to get to.

4. Forgetting to Hit Orbs

Orbs are crucial for progressing through the level. In the 31-36% (Terron) section, you'll navigate a lengthy UFO segment with narrow spaces between spikes. Make sure you hit every orb you come across. Missing an orb can throw off your timing and make the rest of the section much harder. Keep your eyes peeled and your fingers ready!

5. Getting Frustrated

Lastly, don't get frustrated if you don't beat Kenos on your first try. This level is super challenging, and even the best players need practice. Take a break if you need to, and come back with a fresh mind. Remember, every attempt helps you get better. Keep practicing, and you'll conquer Geometry Dash Kenos in no time!
